Frequently Asked Questions
PayDollar Online Payment Gateway

1.1 What are the e-commerce payment acceptance services provided by PayDollar?

There are 2 service plans, namely (a) PayDollar eCommerce Standard Plan and (b) PayDollar eCommerce Premier Plan.

1.2 Who can apply for PayDollar e-Commerce payment services?

We accept applications from any businesses, organizations and charitable bodies registered in Indonesia.

1.3 What are the key components of the various PayDollar e-Commerce payment services?

We offer an one-stop and total payment acceptance solutions, in which we process multi-lingual, multi-currency and multi-channel payment transactions as well as fund settlements. Apart from the comprehensive ranges of tools on user administration, void and refund, transaction reporting, in addition, we provide a wide range of value-added processing features including AppPay, Payment Link, Customized Payment Page, Instalment, PayBooth, Recurring Payment, eVoucher and Anti-fraud.

1.4 What are the benefits of using PayDollar e-Commerce payment services?

Our e-Commerce payment acceptance service is comprehensive, easy-to-implement, convenient, efficient and economical. Here are just a few benefits for PayDollar merchants:

  • Simple and Easy: A simple application (approval given in as soon as 10 days) opens up the door to one-stop payment gateway AND merchant account service. Implementation effort is minimal with no extra hardware or software required.
  • Flexible: Merchants can accept payments through credit/debit cards, net-banking, digital wallets, prepaid cards, buy now pay later, over-the-counter and other digital means from all customers.
  • Cost-efficient: We offer payment services with competitive set-up costs, monthly fees and transaction commission rates. There are no hidden costs like remittance fee or foreign exchange charge. Rebate is available for high-volume businesses.
  • Partnership Opportunities: Merchants may capitalize the e-commerce ready customer base of PayDollar Direct members to grow their businesses.
  • Value-added Services: PayDollar's unique features, like AppPay, Payment Link, Customized Payment Page, Instalment, PayBooth, Recurring Payment and eVoucher can be added to standard service to enhance e-business.

1.5 What kind of payment methods can PayDollar e-commerce merchants accept?

PayDollar merchants may accept world-wide credit card (e.g. American Express, JCB, MasterCard, and Visa).

1.6 In what currency does PayDollar settle payments received by merchants?

Currently, PayDollar e-commerce merchants can accept payments in AUD, BND, CNY, EUR, HKD, IDR, JPY, KRW, MOP, MYR, NZD, PHP, THB, GBP, USD and many more, and range of currencies of Asian countries. Holders of overseas credit cards may also shop at the site, and we settle the transactions in merchant registered currency. The issuing banks of such credit cards convert the payments into their respective currencies and charge the cardholders with such amounts.


1.7 How much do PayDollar e-Commerce payment acceptance services cost?

There are only few standard chargeable items for a merchant. There is an one-off set-up charge, a small monthly service charge and transaction commission based on the payments received and a chargeback handling fee, but no other hidden charges. For details, please contact us at .


1.8 Do I have to pay any charges to other parties besides PayDollar?

For PayDollar e-Commerce plan merchants, there is no addition fee charged by any party other than PayDollar. For PayDollar Premier plan merchants, the selected bank may levy its charges to the merchant. Please note that there may from time to time be contingent charges (e.g. refund processing charge), which may be collected by PayDollar or our partner bank.

Our payment services require possession of a shopping cart on the merchant side. (Please refer to our Technical FAQ session for other shopping cart related questions.). And, we are also pleased to offer our easy-to-setup and cost-effective shopping cart service to address your needs.

3.4 What is a shopping cart?

A shopping cart is a program installed in your web site that enables your customers to view and buy products online. Its service flow resembles that of shopping at a supermarket: place products from a shelf into a basket, and then proceed to checkout to pay for the items purchased.


3.5 What is a payment gateway?

A payment gateway is a software program that is embedded into a merchant's web site for transmitting transaction data to the credit card acquirer, payment service provider or bank for both authorization and settlement purposes.


3.6 Would PayDollar e-Commerce payment acceptance services affect the speed of my web site?

No. There are many factors that could affect the speed of a web site, like the power of the host server, quantity and size of images in the site, search methods and so on.


3.7 How does my web site feed the information from our shopping cart to your payment page, and vice versa?

When a customer places an order at PayDollar merchant's web site and selects the payment method, the customer will be redirected to our payment page for payment processing. All necessary parameters (e.g. transaction amount, order reference number, customer name) will be carried forward to the payment page at the same time. When the payment is processed, the results (either successful or failed) will be shown to the customer in another web page. Such information will also be fed to the designated web page of the merchant. for more sophisticated post-payment processing.


3.8 Does the payment result that PayDollar shows to the customer and the merchant also include information about items purchased by the customer?

In general, we do not handle information other than the parameters necessary for transaction processing. Parameters such as items purchased, model numbers, quantities, shipment addresses and so on, do not fall into this category. Basically merchants may refer to shopping carts for such information. Meanwhile, bundled services will be available to meet specific business needs.

4.5 How does a merchant handle refund to a customer? What are the charges?

The availability of refund service depends on the service plan.

  • PayDollar e-Commerce merchants should submit a completed "Refund Requisition Form" to us, stating details of the transaction to be refunded. There is no refund charges and refund charges will be returned to merchants
  • PayDollar e-Commerce own-merchant-account customers should raise refund requests directly to our partner bank.

In any case, we monitor refund activity of merchants very closely. We are particularly concerned with excessive refunds since such may mean unsatisfactory service level (like late delivery or stock shortage).


4.6 What is a chargeback?

A chargeback is the forced reversal of a transaction by the credit card issuing bank. Chargebacks take place when we receive notification from our partner bank that the cardholder's card issuing bank has received a dispute notice from the cardholder, whose card had been charged by our merchants. In certain cases defence is possible (e.g. order denial while delivery is completed indeed), given merchants are able to provide adequate evidence. Sometimes (for example, fraudulent card usage) a chargeback is made immediately. Please note that excessive chargebacks may result in service terms revision, service suspension or even termination.


4.7 What is a rolling reserve?

The purpose of which is to prepare and maintain a sum for contingent chargeback requests. The rolling reserve is usually a percentage of monthly transactions (which ranges from 10% to 40%), which will be released to merchant after a short period of time (usually 60 days).


4.8 I'm concerned with the issue of online fraud. What should I do to minimize fraud?
Fraudulent behavior online takes various forms from stolen card to system hacking. As an experienced payment service operator, we would like to suggest the following actions to minimize fraud.
  • Order details
    Merchants should urge customers to provide adequate information upon ordering, especially contact information of the customers. Make sure that the customer is a legitimate cardholder. More attention is required for suspicious orders (like remote delivery addresses or simultaneous multiple orders).
  • Free mail address
    Some fraudsters attempt to mask their identity by using a free mail addresses. While most users of free mail addresses are indeed legitimate, caution should be exercised for orders with free mail addresses, especially when this is the only way to contact the customers.
  • Out-of-norm orders
    Merchants should be wary of orders that falls outside usual ordering patterns, like bulk orders or purchases that greatly exceed the average transaction amount.
  • IP record
    Beware of orders made from odd locations (which are sometimes traceable with the IP addresses), especially where credit card fraud is more common.
  • Proof of delivery
    Get a signed proof of delivery or receipt if available upon retrieval request.
  • Return policy
    Merchants should devise and maintain clear, easy to understand and consistent product return policies to keep customers well informed

An effective way of minimizing losses due to fraudulence is to encourage customers to register as PayDollar members. All bank accounts and credit cards of our members are authenticated, meaning that they are virtually "genuine" customers.

5.1 What is Verified by VISA, and American Express SafeKey?

These are the feature instituted by VISA International, MasterCard International and JCB International to protect online and mobile purchasing. It enhances the existing credit card with an additional personal password to be used to authenticate a genuine cardholder purchasing online. When a credit cardholder of these shops online, he/she will be required to enter the password in the same way as entering a PIN at an ATM. That means only the cardholder can use that card. For details, please visit Visa International, VISA International web site.


5.2 Is it compulsory for online and wireless merchant to join Verified by VISA, and American Express SafeKey?

Yes. According to these card organizations, merchant whose engages in business accepting online or mobile credit card payment have to join these over time. As PayDollar merchants, we are pleased to offer this protection service FREE.


5.3 What are the protection benefits?

To merchant:

  • Reduce fraud by assuring cardholder's identity with the authentication of Card Issuer;
  • Increase confidence to ship to the cardholders worldwide, resulting in increased sales;
  • Reduce your liability for fraudulent and disputed purchases.

To cardholder:

  • Reduce risk to release credit card information for online purchasing and avoid unauthorized card use;
  • Enjoy more confidence shopping online with better protection.

5.5 How does Verified by VISA, and American Express SafeKey work?

Please refer to Visa, Mastercard, American Express and JCB for details.

For those merchants who join PayDollar eCommerce Standard plan, there is no need for them to modify their web site. For PayDollar eCommerce Premier plan, the merchants need to install a license which is provided by the acquiring bank.


5.7 How does the merchant know that a transaction is passed Verified by VISA, and American Express SafeKey?

For those transactions processed by any of these, in the PayDollar Merchant Administration site. The payer authentication (Payer Auth) column in your transaction detail report of the transactions will be shown as "T", which means "True".
